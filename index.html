01 / The Method

Four steps, then
everything else is yours.

01

A conversation

We begin with a call — no form funnels, no pressure. I ask about pace, what you love, what you'd rather not do. I listen more than I speak.

Typically 45 min, by video
02

A draft written for you

I sketch a route by hand — regions, camps, people to meet. You get one document, clearly priced, with rationale for every choice. We iterate until it feels right.

Delivered in 3–5 days
03

On the ground, with us

From arrival to departure, someone from our team of twenty-five is with you. Your guide is the same person every day. We carry the logistics so you carry nothing. Our multilingual team includes Russian-speaking support.

Concierge, not operator
04

Flexibility as principle

Want to stay another night because the leopard cubs returned? We move the plan. The itinerary is a starting hypothesis — not a contract.

Plans shift, daily

06 / FAQ

What we're
often asked.

01
How is Skazka different from a regular safari operator?
+
Most operators sell fixed packages and hand you off between a dozen vendors. We run one team — twenty-five people I've trained personally — and the same guide stays with you for the whole trip. There is no package. Every journey is written for the specific guests coming.
02
Who actually leads the trip day-to-day?
+
I do, for most trips — and when I can't, it is one of three senior guides I've worked with for six years or more. You will always know who it is before you arrive, and speak with them directly during planning.
03
What does 'engineer's approach' actually mean in practice?
+
Three things. One — camp logistics are thought through the way I'd design a system: redundancy on vehicles, power, and water; honest risk margins. Two — I weave Kenya's engineering history into the guiding, because you can't understand a migration route without understanding the railway that cut through it. Three — if a storm cuts a road, we route around it rather than cancelling a day.
04
Group size and privacy?
+
We only take small parties — couples, families, and close-friend groups up to six. One vehicle, one guide, one chef. We do not pool strangers. Ever.
05
What is included in the price?
+
Everything after you land: all camps and lodging, all meals, all transfers including charter flights where relevant, park and conservancy fees, the guide and crew, and the concierge throughout. International flights and visas are yours.
06
Can plans change mid-trip?
+
Yes — that is the point. If the leopard cubs have come back and you want another night, we stay. If you've had enough bush and want Lamu two days early, we move. Flexibility is the product.
07
When should I book?
+
Six to twelve months before travel is comfortable. For July–October (Mara migration peak) we are often full a year out. We hold small windows for last-minute guests — ask.
08
What about children?
+
Welcome. I have taken guests as young as five. For children under ten we adjust pace, driving hours, and walking — the trip becomes calmer, not less interesting.
