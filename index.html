Before You Decide

The answers that matter most, first.

Cost, scope, cancellation terms, and safety — the questions that actually determine whether you book.

How much does a Kenya safari cost?

Most trips run $3,000 to $8,000 per person depending on season, camps, route length, and group size. We send a line-item quote once the route outline is agreed — no ballpark-only conversations.

What does Skazka handle?

Everything on the ground in Kenya: route design, camp bookings, KPSGA-certified guides, internal transfers, and daily logistics. We also advise on international flight timing and the Kenya eVisa process.

What is the cancellation policy?

30% deposit to confirm. Full refund if cancelled 60+ days before arrival. 50% refund at 30–59 days. Under 30 days, the deposit is non-refundable. We recommend travel insurance and can suggest providers.

How is safety handled?

All guides are KPSGA-certified (Kenya Professional Safari Guides Association). Vehicles carry first-aid kits and satellite phones. We partner with AMREF Flying Doctors for emergency medical evacuation across all routes.

A Day On Safari

What a Mara day actually looks like.

Not a brochure version — the real shape of a game-drive day, from first light to camp dinner.

  1. 05:45

    Out before sunrise

    Coffee at camp, then straight onto the plains. Lions and cheetahs hunt early — this is the window your guide is watching for. 2–3 hour morning drive.

  2. 09:00

    Bush breakfast, then a decision

    Your guide sets up breakfast by the vehicle. You decide: keep driving, head to a hippo pool, or return to camp. Children under 8 usually choose camp and the pool.

  3. 16:00

    Afternoon drive, golden light

    The herds move again. Elephants at watering holes, leopards in the trees. Photographers get the light they came for. 2–3 hours, flexible return.

  4. 19:30

    Camp dinner, guide debrief

    Three-course dinner. Your guide talks through tomorrow's plan — where the migration is moving, which crossing points look active, what the weather means for routes.

How It Works

Route first, camps second, budget always visible.

Most operators sell you a camp and build a route around it. We design the route first, then match camps to the rhythm of the trip.

01 / Route Design

Built for your group, not a template

A family with young children gets shorter drives and camps with pools. A photography couple gets early access and extended sightings. The route changes — the quality doesn't.

02 / Camp Selection

Fewer moves, better-matched camps

We work with 30+ camps across the Mara, Amboseli, Laikipia, and Samburu. We pick camps for the experience they create on that specific night of the trip — not brand loyalty.

03 / Transparent Budget

Line-item quote, no hidden costs

You see what each camp costs, what the guide fees are, what the transfers run. If the total needs adjusting, we show you the tradeoffs — not a vague "we can make it work."
